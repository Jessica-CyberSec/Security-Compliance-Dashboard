🔐 Password Hygiene for Cashiers

🎯 Master strong passwords and keep POS & inventory systems safe.

Role: Cashier • Estimated time: 4–6 minutes Beginner

📌 Objective

Help cashiers create strong, secure passwords and follow safe password management practices on POS terminals, tablets, and back-office systems.

🔑 Why Passwords Matter

🔒 Your password is the front door key to customer data, payments, and stock info. A weak or reused password can lead to account compromise, fraudulent refunds, or inventory tampering.

💥 Common Mistakes

  • Using easy passwords like 123456 or password
  • Reusing the same password across apps
  • Writing passwords on sticky notes near the register
  • Sharing passwords with coworkers or over chat

✔️ Best Practices

  • Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Create unique passwords for each system (POS ≠ Email)
  • Change passwords after suspicious activity or when prompted
  • Enable MFA if available for cashier logins

📲 Tools

  • Use company‑approved password managers to store credentials
  • Let the manager or IT reset access—don’t share codes
  • Lock the screen when stepping away from the register

🎯 Final Reminder

One weak password can unlock the entire store network. Keep it strong, keep it secret, and lock your screen every time you step away.
