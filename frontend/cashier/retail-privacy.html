🛍️ Retail Data Privacy Basics

🔒 Protect customer info at the register, back office, and everywhere in between.

Role: Cashier • Estimated time: 4–6 minutes Essential

📌 Objective

Train cashiers to protect customer data and understand privacy rules impacting daily retail operations.

📦 What Data Are We Talking About?

🎫 Payment & Identity

  • Credit/debit card numbers (PAN), expiration dates
  • Customer names and addresses
  • Email addresses and phone numbers

🧾 Shopping Details

  • Purchase history and returns
  • Loyalty IDs and rewards activity
  • Gift card balances and receipts with PII

🗃️ Sensitive Bits

  • Any document or screen showing personal data
  • Photos of IDs or receipts
  • Support tickets with contact info

🚫 What You Should Never Do

  • Leave screens unlocked at the register or service desk
  • Discuss customer information out loud or in public areas
  • Print or write down card numbers or CVV codes
  • Share login credentials with coworkers (ever)

🛡️ How to Stay Compliant

  • Always log out or lock screens when stepping away
  • Follow store policy for handling receipts and masked PAN
  • Use only approved apps and devices for customer data
  • Report lost/stolen devices and suspicious access immediately
📣 Remember: Privacy = trust. Handle data as if it were your own. When unsure, ask a supervisor before proceeding.

🎯 Final Reminder

Protecting privacy is part of excellent customer service — and it’s also the law. Small actions (like locking screens) make a big difference.
