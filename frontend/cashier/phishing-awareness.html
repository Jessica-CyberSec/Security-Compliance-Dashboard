🛡️ Phishing Awareness Training for Cashiers

🎯 Learn to spot scams, avoid traps, and report incidents fast.

Role: Cashier • Estimated time: 5–7 minutes Essential

📌 Objective

Equip cashiers with practical knowledge and instincts to identify, avoid, and report phishing attempts targeting retail operations.

🚨 What Is Phishing?

🎭 Phishing is when attackers impersonate trusted sources (banks, delivery firms, even your manager) to steal passwords, payment info, or install malware via links and attachments.

🧠 Real-Life Scenarios

  • “Urgent payment needed” email with a subtly misspelled sender address.
  • Fake tech support call asking for POS credentials or remote access.
  • Suspicious attachment from “the boss” with a vague subject line.

🔍 Spot the Red Flags

  • Unsolicited messages demanding urgent action or payment
  • Typos, odd grammar, or unfamiliar sender domains
  • Links that look legit but open strange URLs
  • Unusual requests from internal contacts (e.g., gift card purchases)

💡 Tips for Cashiers

  • Verify first — when unsure, ask your manager in person.
  • Never share passwords or codes via email, text, or phone.
  • Slow down — urgency is a tactic; think before you click.
  • Lock screens whenever leaving the register.
  • Report suspicious items immediately to your supervisor or IT.

✅ If You're Targeted

  1. Do not reply to the message.
  2. Do not click links or open attachments.
  3. Take a screenshot or photo if possible.
  4. Report directly to your supervisor or cybersecurity contact.

🗣️ Roleplay Exercise

Coworker: “I got an email from ‘VendorInvo1ce@payfast.co’ asking for a quick transfer. What should I do?”
You: “Let’s verify with our supervisor first — it could be phishing.”

🎯 Final Reminder

Scammers rely on speed and panic. Slow down, verify, and report — one smart move can prevent a costly incident.
